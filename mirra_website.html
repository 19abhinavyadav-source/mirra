Mirra's non-negotiable rules

What Mirra will never do

These aren't bugs. They're the whole point.

1
✍️
Never write your essay
Not one sentence. Not a thesis statement. Not an "example structure." If Mirra wrote it, it's not yours — and it won't pass an IB examiner who has read 10,000 essays and knows exactly what authentic student thinking looks like.
2
👍
Never validate your argument
"Is this argument good?" is the wrong question. Mirra will never say "yes, that's correct" or "no, that's wrong." Instead it will ask: "What would someone who disagrees say?" The point is for you to evaluate your own argument.
3
🔄
Never ask the same question twice
Mirra rotates through seven question types and tracks which it has used in your session. If you're stuck, Mirra changes angle — not angle of the same question, a genuinely different dimension of your thinking.
4
😤
Never ignore emotional signals
"I'm bored." "Just tell me." "This is pointless." Mirra detects these signals and stops the Socratic arc immediately. It consolidates what you figured out, closes warmly, and offers to move on. Forcing thinking on a frustrated student produces nothing.
5
📝
Never ask two questions at once
One question. Every response. Without exception. Two questions let you pick the easier one and avoid the hard one. One question forces you to go somewhere you might have dodged.
6
🏆
Never lower the standard
Mirra treats you as a thinker, not a student to be managed. The questions will be hard. The standard will be IB standard. But the warmth and patience are infinite — Mirra never makes you feel stupid for not knowing something yet.

Questions

The honest answers.

Will Mirra write my essay for me?
No. That's the point. Mirra will never write a sentence of your essay, argument, or conclusion. An IB examiner has read 10,000 essays and immediately recognises AI-written work — and a 1 on IB's academic integrity scale. Mirra builds the thinking behind the essay. You write the essay.
What does a "question" count as?
Each Socratic exchange with the main Claude Sonnet model counts as one question. Classification, gap detection, and morning check-ins run on a faster model and don't count against your quota. Questions reset on the 1st of every month.
Can I use Mirra for all IB subjects?
Yes. Mirra adapts its questioning approach to your specific subjects — empirical for Sciences, interpretive for Literature, causal for History, proof-based for Maths. The Socratic method applies across all knowledge areas, including TOK.
How is this different from ChatGPT or Claude?
Generic AI gives you answers. Mirra never gives answers. The system prompt is built specifically around IB Socratic pedagogy, with five decision steps, seven question types, and emotional signal detection. It's not a chatbot — it's a purpose-built thinking companion for the IB.
Is my conversation data private?
Yes. Your conversation data is stored securely and never shared with third parties. You can export all your data at any time, and a full account deletion removes everything from our systems including reasoning gap history and session transcripts.
Does Mirra work for IGCSE and A-Levels too?
Yes. Mirra has a "short arc" mode for IGCSE and A-Level exam preparation — 3 to 4 exchanges to probe the core idea, then a bridge to application. The same question types apply, but depth is calibrated for the assessment style.
Can I cancel anytime?
Yes. No lock-in, no penalties. Cancel before your next billing date and you keep access until the end of your paid period. Your reasoning gap history and session transcripts are preserved even after cancellation.
I'm a parent. Should I sign my child up?
Mirra is designed for students aged 15–19 working on IB and IGCSE curricula. The platform builds genuine intellectual independence — the opposite of academic dependency. If you want your child to develop real thinking skills, not just better grades, Mirra is designed for exactly that.

Four principles

What we believe
about learning.

These aren't product features. They're the convictions that determine every decision we make about what Mirra should and shouldn't do.

1
Understanding cannot be transferred. It must be constructed.
You cannot give a student understanding the way you can give them information. Understanding is built, by the student, through the act of working something out. The teacher's job — and Mirra's job — is not to shortcut that process. It's to make the process possible.
2
The right question is worth a hundred explanations.
Explanations create the illusion of understanding. The student hears the teacher's reasoning and mistakes it for their own. One precisely-aimed question — one that forces the student to reason from their own premises — creates more intellectual progress than an hour of lecture.
3
Academic integrity is not a rule. It's a precondition for learning.
When a student submits AI-written work as their own, they don't just break a rule. They cheat themselves. They lose the only chance they had to actually work something out. Mirra refuses to write because it is, at its core, on the student's side.
4
Intellectual warmth and intellectual rigour are not opposites.
The best teachers are warm and demanding at the same time. They make you feel like a thinker even when you're wrong. They push hard on your assumptions while making you feel respected. Mirra is designed to hold this tension — never preachy, never harsh, always precise.
